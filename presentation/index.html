An AI institution that doesn't just advise — it acts.

Live verification. Adaptive re-routing. Fiduciary charter checks. Built for what chatbots structurally cannot do.

The Problem

Healthcare navigation is broken for patients

Hours of phone calls

Patients call insurers, then clinics, then insurers again — chasing coverage answers that contradict each other.

Stale information

Portals and chatbots repeat what should be true from training data — not what is true for you, today.

Generic advice

Recommendations ignore your priorities: cost, wait time, avoiding surgery, keeping your doctor.

Hidden contradictions

Your insurer says a clinic is in-network. The clinic says they dropped your plan last month. You find out at checkout.

Our Solution

The first Autonomous Patient Organization

APO is a multi-agent system that orchestrates parallel live verification, detects contradictions in real time, adapts its plan, and checks every recommendation against your stated priorities.

Acts, not advises

Fires concurrent calls to insurers and providers — the work you'd spend hours doing.

Adapts live

When sources disagree, it detects the conflict and re-routes automatically.

Fiduciary by design

Every recommendation is checked against your Patient Charter — ranked priorities you define.

"A chat model tells you what should be true. APO finds out what is true — right now, for you."

Voice ExecutorLive transcript of the call that revealed the contradiction
Demo Scenario

Sleep apnea — end to end

Patient context

  • Diagnosis: Obstructive sleep apnea (physician-provided)
  • Plan: BlueCross Silver PPO
  • Records: Moderate severity, hypertension, tried CPAP
  • Goal: Sleep study + CPAP pathway, minimize cost

Planted contradiction

Insurer directory lists Metro Pulmonary & Sleep (Clinic B) as in-network.

Clinic B receptionist confirms they dropped the plan in March 2026.

Scenario is deterministic in mock data — the mechanism generalizes to real verification.

APO never diagnoses. Diagnosis is input from the doctor. Records only personalize downstream research.

Key design decisions

  • Parallel agents via asyncio.gather — same timestamps prove concurrency
  • Contradiction planted in JSON mock world for reliable demos
  • Distinct agent personas, single LLM — fast to build, easy to extend
  • Stub mode works without API key — always demoable

For patients

What we explicitly don't do (if judges ask)

  • No binding decisions or form submission — draft plan for patient approval
  • No real PHI in demo — synthetic patient data only
  • Voice calls are investigative, not authorizing
